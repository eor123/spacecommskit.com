What We Build

Engineering Services

Four core service areas — each built on the same RF platform foundation. Engagements can be scoped individually or combined into a full mission package.

SERVICE 01

Custom PC-104 Payload Board Design

Need a payload board that fits the CubeSat PC-104 stack and talks to the SCK-2400 comms board? We design it. Sensor integration, power management, expansion headers, RF shielding — the full hardware stack designed to your mission specifications and assembled in Tennessee.

✓ 4-layer KiCad PCB design to PC-104 standard
✓ Sensor integration — I2C, SPI, UART, analog
✓ Power regulation and protection circuits
✓ RF shielding and EMI layout practice
✓ Gerbers, BOM, and assembly documentation
Typical engagement: $5,000 – $15,000
SERVICE 02

SCK-2400 Integration & Firmware

You have a payload. You need it to communicate over the SCK-2400 RF link with your ground station. We write the firmware — CC2652P SimpleLink SDK, CCSDS framing, custom command protocols, payload data pipelines — and extend the ground station software to receive and display your data.

✓ CC2652P firmware on TI SimpleLink SDK
✓ CCSDS packet framing and telemetry pipeline
✓ Custom command and response protocol
✓ Ground station C# extension panels
✓ Heavily commented, documented codebase
Typical engagement: $8,000 – $25,000
SERVICE 03

Full CubeSat Comms Stack

From PC-104 payload board design through RF firmware through ground station software — delivered as a complete, tested, documented communications stack for your CubeSat mission. This is the full engagement: hardware, firmware, software, and the documentation your team needs to own it.

✓ Everything in Services 01 + 02
✓ Integration testing on assembled hardware
✓ RF performance verification — TinySA measured
✓ Complete developer guide and decision log
✓ Source code transfer and knowledge handoff
Typical engagement: $20,000 – $60,000
SERVICE 04

Mission Support Retainer

Ongoing engineering support through your integration, test, and launch phases. Firmware updates, ground station modifications, anomaly investigation, pre-launch RF verification — a dedicated engineering resource available on a monthly retainer basis through your mission lifecycle.

✓ Firmware updates and bug resolution
✓ Ground station feature additions
✓ Pre-launch RF performance verification
✓ Integration support and anomaly response
✓ Priority response during launch window
Typical engagement: $2,000 – $5,000 / month
Who This Is For

The Right Fit

🎓

Universities & Research Labs

CubeSat programs that need a validated RF communications solution without building an in-house RF team. We understand academic timelines, grant budget structures, and the publication requirements that come with research hardware.

🚀

Small Satellite Companies

Early-stage teams building their first or second CubeSat mission who need custom comms hardware faster than an internal hire can deliver it. Flight-ready hardware from a team that has already made the expensive mistakes.

🏛️

Government & Defense Adjacent

Programs requiring US-sourced components, US engineering, and full documentation traceability. We source exclusively from authorized US distributors and maintain complete decision logs on every engagement.

🔬

HAB & Stratospheric Research

Researchers who have outgrown off-the-shelf HAB trackers and need mission-specific sensor integration, custom telemetry pipelines, and ground station software tailored to their data collection requirements.

⚡
Not the right fit?

If your project needs large-volume production, radiation-hardened space-grade components, or DO-178C certified software — we are not the right partner and we will tell you that upfront. We work best on small-batch, research-grade, and early-flight hardware where boutique engineering adds more value than a large contractor's overhead.

How It Works

1

Conversation

Tell us about your mission — what you need to measure, transmit, and receive, your timeline, and your budget range. No NDA required at this stage. We give you an honest assessment of fit within 48 hours.
